## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Craftsman Jig Saws

Choosing the right jig saw involves more than just picking the one with the most power. Consider these factors to ensure you get a tool that perfectly matches your needs.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, assess what you'll be cutting. Are you working primarily with thin plywood for crafts, or will you be tackling thick hardwood, metal, or plastics? The materials dictate the power and features you need. Also, consider your environment. If you work in various locations or far from an outlet, a cordless model from the Craftsman V20 line is ideal. If you're in a dedicated workshop with constant power needs, a corded model might be more reliable and cost-effective.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Craftsman jig saws offer a range of price points. A bare-tool cordless model can start around $60-$80. If you need a full kit with a battery and charger, expect to pay between $100 and $150. High-performance brushless models will be at the top of that range. Corded models are often the most budget-friendly, typically falling in the $50-$90 range. Set your budget based on your frequency of use and the importance of premium features like brushless motors or included batteries.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
- **Variable Speed:** Essential for control. Slower speeds are for starting cuts and cutting metal, while faster speeds are for wood. Look for a range from 0 to at least 2,500 SPM (Strokes Per Minute).
- **Orbital Action:** This feature moves the blade in a slight circular motion, which clears sawdust and allows for faster, more aggressive cuts in wood. More settings (e.g., 4 vs. 3) offer finer control.
- **Tool-less Blade Change:** A huge convenience feature that lets you swap blades quickly without needing an Allen wrench.
- **Bevel Capability:** The ability for the saw's base (shoe) to tilt, usually up to 45 degrees, is crucial for making angled cuts.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Look for a jig saw with a solid metal base plate (shoe) for stability and durability. A stamped steel shoe is standard, but cast metal is more robust. The handle should have a comfortable, non-slip rubber overmold to reduce vibration and improve grip. For cordless models, a brushless motor is a sign of higher quality and longer tool life.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Craftsman has a long-standing reputation for producing reliable tools for homeowners and DIY enthusiasts. Sticking with an established brand like Craftsman ensures better product support, warranty coverage (often 3 years), and a wide ecosystem of compatible tools and accessories, like the V20 battery system.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Cordless jig saws offer maximum portability but require charged batteries. Corded models have unlimited runtime but restrict your movement. Consider the tool's weight, especially if you'll be using it for long periods. Also, think about storage. Most Craftsman tools are compatible with their VERSATRACK wall organization system, which is a great space-saving solution.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy craftsman jig saws?

Investing in a Craftsman jig saw is a wise decision for a wide range of users, from homeowners tackling weekend projects to serious woodworking hobbyists. The primary value proposition lies in the brand's successful blend of performance, reliability, and affordability. Unlike some professional-grade brands that come with a steep price tag, or budget brands that may sacrifice durability, Craftsman hits a sweet spot. For the price, you get robust features like variable speed control, orbital settings, and bevel capabilities that are essential for quality work.

Compared to using a handsaw for curved cuts, a jig saw is exponentially faster, more accurate, and less physically demanding. When compared to other power saws, its unique ability to make intricate, curved, and beveled cuts in various materials makes it an indispensable and versatile tool. The long-term benefits are clear: you'll complete projects faster, achieve a more professional finish, and have a reliable tool that, with proper care, will last for years. ## What Is the Best Way to Choose craftsman jig saws?

Choosing the best Craftsman jig saw is a straightforward process if you follow a few key steps.

1.  **Assess Your Power Source:** The first decision is cordless vs. corded. If you value mobility and already own or plan to buy other Craftsman V20 tools, a cordless model is the clear winner. If you need uninterrupted power for long sessions in a workshop and want to save a bit of money, a corded model is an excellent choice.

2.  **Evaluate Your Workload:** Be honest about your project types. For occasional home repairs and light crafts, a standard brushed cordless model (like the CMCS600B) or a corded model (CMES610) is more than sufficient. If you are a serious hobbyist who frequently cuts dense hardwoods or works for long hours, investing in a brushless model (CMCS650B) will pay off in performance and longevity.

3.  **Consider a Kit vs. Bare Tool:** If you're new to the V20 system, buying a kit (like the CMCS600D1) that includes a battery and charger is the most economical way to start. If you already have V20 batteries, you can save money by purchasing the "bare tool" only.

4.  **Don't Forget Blades:** The best saw is useless without the right blade. Jig saw blades are specific to the material you're cutting (wood, metal, plastic, laminate). Always have a variety pack of high-quality T-shank or U-shank blades on hand to match your project.

**Common Mistake to Avoid:** A common error is buying the most powerful, feature-rich saw when your needs are simple. This leads to overspending. Conversely, don't buy the most basic model if you plan to tackle demanding projects, as you'll only end up frustrated. Match the tool to your realistic, intended use.

## FAQ

**What is the best craftsman jig saws for beginners?**
The best option for beginners is the CRAFTSMAN V20 Cordless Jig Saw Kit (CMCS600D1). It includes the saw, a battery, and a charger, providing everything you need to start. The saw itself is user-friendly, with variable speed and orbital settings that are easy to understand and use, offering a great balance of performance and simplicity without being overwhelming.

**How much should I spend on craftsman jig saws?**
You should expect to spend between $60 and $150. A reliable corded model can be found for around $70. A bare-tool cordless model typically costs about $80, while a full cordless kit with a battery and charger usually falls in the $120 to $150 range. Spending more often gets you a more powerful brushless motor and longer runtime.

**What features are most important in craftsman jig saws?**
The most important features are a variable speed trigger for control, orbital settings to adjust cutting aggressiveness, and a beveling shoe for making angled cuts. A tool-less blade change mechanism is also a highly valuable convenience feature that saves a lot of time and hassle during projects.

**How do I maintain my craftsman jig saws?**
Maintenance is simple. Regularly blow out sawdust from the motor vents with compressed air to prevent overheating. Keep the base plate clean and check it for squareness. Occasionally, apply a drop of light machine oil to the blade guide roller. Always store the tool in a clean, dry place to prevent rust.

**Are expensive craftsman jig saws worth it?**
Yes, if your work demands it. More expensive models, like those with brushless motors, offer tangible benefits: more power, longer battery life, and a longer overall tool lifespan. For a professional or a very active hobbyist, the improved efficiency and durability justify the higher cost. For a casual user, a standard model is perfectly adequate.

**What brands make the best craftsman jig saws?**
Craftsman is the brand that makes Craftsman jig saws. They have built a strong reputation over decades for producing durable and reliable tools for the consumer market. While other brands like DeWalt, Makita, and Bosch also make excellent jig saws, Craftsman focuses on providing great value and performance for DIYers and homeowners.

**How long do craftsman jig saws typically last?**
With proper care and for typical DIY use, a Craftsman jig saw can easily last for 5 to 10 years, or even longer. The lifespan depends heavily on the frequency of use and the types of materials being cut. A brushless model will generally have a longer service life than a standard brushed motor due to having fewer wearable parts.

**Can I find good craftsman jig saws on a budget?**
Absolutely. The CRAFTSMAN 5 Amp Corded Jig Saw (CMES610) offers excellent performance at a very budget-friendly price. For an even better deal, consider looking for a renewed version of the same model, which provides the same functionality at a significant discount, making it a fantastic value.

**What's the difference between T-shank and U-shank blades?**
The shank is the end of the blade that locks into the saw. T-shank blades have a tang at the top and are the modern standard, used in most saws with a tool-less blade change system. U-shank blades have a U-shaped cutout. Most modern Craftsman jig saws are great because they accept both types, giving you maximum flexibility when buying blades.

**Do I need a brushless motor in my jig saw?**
You don't necessarily need one, but it's a great upgrade. A brushless motor is more efficient, which means it can deliver more power and get longer runtime from the same battery. It also runs cooler and has a longer lifespan. If you use your jig saw frequently or for tough jobs, the extra investment in a brushless model is worthwhile.
