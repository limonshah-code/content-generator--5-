## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Mattress At Ashley Furniture

Choosing a mattress is a significant decision that impacts your daily well-being. Before you buy, consider these crucial factors to ensure you select the perfect mattress for your unique needs.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Your primary sleep position is the most important factor. Side sleepers generally need a softer mattress to cushion shoulders and hips, while back and stomach sleepers require a firmer surface for spinal alignment. Consider who will be using the bed. If you share with a partner, look for excellent motion isolation, a feature common in memory foam and hybrid mattresses with wrapped coils. Also, think about your body temperature. If you tend to sleep hot, a cooling gel-infused or hybrid mattress with good airflow is essential.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Signature Design by Ashley offers a wide range of mattresses, with prices generally falling between $300 and $1,200. A quality queen-size memory foam or basic hybrid will typically be in the $400-$700 range. Premium models with advanced features like higher coil counts, thicker profiles, and specialized cooling foams can cost upwards of $800. Set a realistic budget, but remember that a mattress is a long-term investment in your health. A durable mattress with a 10-year warranty provides great value over its lifespan.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look closely at the mattress type. **Memory Foam** offers excellent pressure relief and motion isolation. **Hybrid** mattresses combine innerspring coils for support and bounce with foam layers for comfort, offering a balanced feel. Pay attention to the profile (thickness), with 12-14 inch models often providing more comfort layers. For hybrids, a higher coil count (800+) generally indicates better support and durability. For foam, look for CertiPUR-US certification, which ensures the material is safe and non-toxic.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
A 10-year warranty, standard on most of these mattresses, is a good indicator of the manufacturer's confidence in their product. The quality of materials is key. For foam mattresses, high-density support foam in the base layer prevents premature sagging. For hybrids, individually wrapped coils are more durable and effective than older, interconnected spring systems. Strong edge support, often created by reinforced perimeter coils, allows you to use the entire mattress surface and prevents the feeling of rolling off.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Choosing a mattress from a well-known brand like Signature Design by Ashley provides a level of assurance. They have a long-standing reputation in the furniture industry and established customer service channels. This means you are more likely to have a straightforward experience with delivery, setup, and any potential warranty claims. Reading customer reviews for specific models can also provide insight into real-world performance and longevity.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
All the mattresses reviewed here come compressed and rolled in a box. This mattress-in-a-box model makes delivery and setup incredibly convenient, as you can easily navigate it through tight hallways and staircases. However, once unboxed, they are heavy and cumbersome to move. Be sure to measure your bedroom to choose the right size (Full, Queen, King) that allows for comfortable movement around the bed. Remember to account for the 48-72 hour expansion period where the mattress needs space to fully decompress.
