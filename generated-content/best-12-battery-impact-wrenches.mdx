## What to Look for Before Buying the Best 1.2 Battery Impact Wrenches

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before purchasing, consider if you are working on a passenger car, a large truck, or industrial machinery. A tool with 300-400 ft-lbs is great for home cars, but you will need 800+ ft-lbs for heavy-duty rusted suspension or truck tires. Also, check if you already own batteries for a specific brand like Ryobi, which can save you money.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($80-$250 ranges)
Budget models often come as 'tool only' or with smaller batteries. For a complete kit with two 4.0Ah batteries and a fast charger, expect to pay between $120 and $180. Professional industrial-grade kits with extra protection and maximum torque can reach the $200-$250 range.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Torque is king, but RPM and IPM (Impacts Per Minute) determine how fast the job gets done. Look for a brushless motor, as it provides better efficiency and longevity. Adjustable speed modes are also vital to avoid over-tightening smaller bolts.

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Check for materials like tungsten steel anvils and aluminum alloy heads. Tools with rubberized 'armor' or drop protection are better for rough garage floors. An oil-resistant grip is also a major plus for automotive work.

### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers

To choose the right wrench, start by checking your vehicle's torque requirements. Look up the lug nut specs; if they require 100 ft-lbs, you want a wrench that offers at least 300 ft-lbs to handle rusted or over-tightened bolts.
