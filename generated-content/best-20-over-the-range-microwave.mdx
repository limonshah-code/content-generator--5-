## What to Look for Before Buying the Best 20 Over The Range Microwave

Choosing the right over-the-range (OTR) microwave is a significant decision that impacts your daily kitchen routine and the overall aesthetic of your space. To ensure you make a choice you'll be happy with for years, consider these crucial factors before you buy.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you start shopping, assess your kitchen and your cooking habits. The vast majority of OTR microwaves are designed to fit a standard 30-inch-wide cabinet opening. Measure your space carefully, including width, height, and depth, to ensure a proper fit. Consider the vertical clearance above your cooktop; if it's limited, a slim-profile model might be the best solution. Think about your cooking style. Do you just need a basic appliance for reheating and defrosting, or would you benefit from advanced features like convection cooking, air frying, or sensor reheat? Matching the microwave's capabilities to your actual needs will prevent you from overspending on features you'll never use.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Over-the-range microwaves come in a wide range of prices. It's helpful to set a budget before you begin your search.
- **Entry-level ($200 - $350):** These models typically offer standard microwave functions, 900-1000 watts of power, and a basic two-speed 300-CFM vent fan. They are reliable for everyday tasks like reheating, defrosting, and making popcorn.
- **Mid-range ($350 - $550):** In this range, you'll find microwaves with more advanced features like sensor cooking, more powerful ventilation, larger capacities, and more sophisticated designs with touch controls.
- **Premium ($550+):** High-end models offer multi-functional capabilities such as convection baking, air frying, and grilling. They often feature more powerful ventilation (400+ CFM), smart home integration, and premium finishes.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Focus on the specs that have the biggest impact on performance.
- **Wattage:** Look for a microwave with at least 1000 watts for fast, even cooking. Lower wattage models can take longer to heat food.
- **Ventilation (CFM):** CFM stands for Cubic Feet per Minute and measures the fan's power. A standard electric range needs at least 300 CFM. If you have a professional-style gas range, or do a lot of high-heat cooking, look for 400 CFM or more.
- **Sensor Cooking:** This feature uses sensors to detect steam and humidity, automatically adjusting the cooking time and power level. It takes the guesswork out of reheating leftovers and cooking vegetables.
- **Turntable:** A rotating turntable is essential for even cooking. Check for the ability to turn it off, which is useful for large, rectangular dishes.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
An OTR microwave is a long-term investment. Look for a sturdy build with a high-quality finish, like fingerprint-resistant stainless steel, that will be easy to clean and stand up to daily use. Pay attention to the handle; a solid, well-attached handle is a good indicator of overall build quality. The interior finish matters, too. Enamel or ceramic interiors are often easier to clean than standard painted ones. Reading user reviews can provide valuable insight into the long-term durability of a specific model.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Opting for a microwave from a well-established brand like GE, Frigidaire, BLACK+DECKER, or Midea often means better product reliability, customer service, and easier access to replacement parts if needed. These companies have years of experience and a reputation to uphold. While newer brands can offer great value, established manufacturers provide an extra layer of confidence in your purchase. Look for models with strong warranties, like the two-year warranty offered by Empava, as this signals the company's faith in its product's longevity.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
While OTR microwaves are not portable, their size is a critical consideration. The standard width is 30 inches to match the most common range and cabinet size. However, height can vary. Standard models are around 16-17 inches tall, while "slim" or "low-profile" models can be as short as 10-12 inches, preserving more space above your cooktop. Always double-check the manufacturer's required installation clearances to ensure proper ventilation and safe operation.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy 20 over the range microwave?

Investing in a 20 over the range microwave is an exceptionally wise decision for most modern kitchens. Its primary value proposition is its brilliant space-saving design. By combining two essential appliances—a microwave and a range hood—into a single unit, it frees up valuable counter space that would otherwise be occupied by a bulky countertop model. This creates a more open, organized, and efficient cooking area, which is a significant advantage in kitchens of any size, but especially in smaller ones.

Compared to the alternative of a separate countertop microwave and a dedicated range hood, the OTR microwave offers a more integrated and streamlined aesthetic. It creates a cohesive look with your other major appliances. Furthermore, the cost of a quality OTR microwave is often comparable to, or even less than, the combined cost of purchasing a good countertop microwave and a separate, effective ventilation hood.

The long-term benefits extend beyond just space and aesthetics. Modern OTR microwaves are packed with advanced technology. Features like sensor cooking, convection capabilities, and even air frying are now common, expanding your cooking versatility without adding more clutter. The integrated ventilation system, while not always as powerful as a dedicated professional hood, is more than sufficient for the average home cook, effectively removing steam, smoke, and cooking odors to improve kitchen air quality. Addressing cost concerns, the value provided in terms of functionality, space efficiency, and improved kitchen environment makes it a smart, long-lasting investment for your home.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose 20 over the range microwave?

Choosing the best 20 over the range microwave requires a methodical approach to ensure you get a model that fits your space, budget, and cooking style. Follow this step-by-step guide for a confident purchase.

**1. Measure Your Space Meticulously:**
Before anything else, measure the width of the cabinet opening where the microwave will be installed. The standard is 30 inches. Also, measure the height from the bottom of the cabinet to your cooktop surface and the depth of your cabinets. This will determine if you need a standard-height or a low-profile model.

**2. Define Your Primary Needs:**
Be honest about how you'll use the microwave.
- **Basic User:** If you primarily reheat, defrost, and make popcorn, a standard 1000-watt model with simple controls will suffice.
- **Enthusiast Cook:** If you want to bake, roast, or achieve crispy results, prioritize models with convection, air fry, or combination cooking modes.
- **Busy Family:** Look for features like sensor reheat, large capacity (1.7 cu. ft. or more), and easy-to-use one-touch buttons.

**3. Set Your Budget:**
Decide how much you are willing to spend. Use the price ranges in our buying guide to understand what features are available at each level. This will help you narrow down your options significantly.

**4. Compare Core Performance Features:**
- **Ventilation (CFM):** 300 CFM is the standard. If you have a gas stove or do a lot of searing, consider a model with 400 CFM or higher for better fume extraction.
- **Wattage:** Aim for 1000W or more for efficient cooking.
- **Special Features:** Decide if sensor cooking, smart controls, or a zero-clearance door are must-haves for your lifestyle.

**5. Read Reviews and Check Warranties:**
Look at user reviews for insights on long-term reliability and real-world performance. A strong warranty, like a 2-year plan, is a great indicator of a manufacturer's confidence in their product.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- **Ignoring Ventilation Needs:** Buying a microwave with an underpowered fan for your cooking style will result in a smoky, smelly kitchen.
- **Forgetting to Measure:** Nothing is worse than buying an appliance that doesn't fit. Measure twice!
- **Paying for Unused Features:** Don't get swayed by a 6-in-1 model if you only ever plan to reheat coffee.

**Upgrade vs. Budget:**
If your current microwave works but you're remodeling, it's the perfect time to upgrade to a model with features like air frying or better ventilation. If you're on a tight budget and just need a replacement, focus on reliable brands that offer the core functions you need without the expensive extras.

## FAQ

**What is the best 20 over the range microwave for beginners?**
For beginners, the best choice is a model with simple, intuitive controls and useful presets. The **BLACK+DECKER 1.7 cu. ft. OTR Microwave** is an excellent option. **How much should I spend on 20 over the range microwave?**
A good quality, reliable over-the-range microwave can be found in the $250 to $450 range. This will typically get you 1000 watts of power, a 300-CFM vent, and useful features like sensor cooking. Models with premium features like air frying, convection, or smart technology can cost $500 or more. Setting a budget in the $300-$400 range will give you access to many excellent, feature-rich options.

**What features are most important in 20 over the range microwave?**
The three most important features are adequate wattage (1000W+ for efficiency), effective ventilation (at least 300 CFM), and a size that fits your space (usually 30 inches wide). After that, sensor cooking is a highly valuable feature that prevents overcooking. Finally, consider interior capacity based on the size of the dishes you typically use.

**How do I maintain my 20 over the range microwave?**
Maintenance is key to longevity. Wipe up spills immediately with a damp cloth. Regularly clean the interior with a microwave-safe cleaner or a solution of water and vinegar. Most importantly, clean or replace the grease filters underneath the microwave every 3-6 months, depending on your cooking frequency. If your model recirculates air, you'll also need to change the charcoal filter periodically.

**Are expensive 20 over the range microwave worth it?**
Expensive models are worth it if you will consistently use their advanced features. If you love baking and want a second oven, a convection microwave is a great investment. If you want to eat healthier and declutter your counter, a model with a built-in air fryer is worth the premium. However, if you only need basic heating and defrosting, a more affordable model is the smarter financial choice.

**What brands make the best 20 over the range microwave?**
Several brands have a strong reputation for quality and reliability. GE, Frigidaire, and BLACK+DECKER are well-established names known for durable and effective appliances. Brands like Midea and Empava are also gaining popularity by offering innovative features, smart technology, and competitive pricing, making them excellent choices as well.

**How long do 20 over the range microwave typically last?**
An over-the-range microwave typically has a lifespan of about 7 to 10 years. This can vary based on usage, brand quality, and how well it is maintained. Regular cleaning, especially of the ventilation filters, can help extend the life of the appliance. The magnetron, which generates the microwaves, is often the first major component to fail.

**Can I find good 20 over the range microwave on a budget?**
Absolutely. You can find excellent OTR microwaves for under $300. Look for models like the **Empava OTR Microwave** which offers fantastic features like sensor cooking and an extended warranty at a very competitive price. Budget-friendly options may not have stainless steel finishes or convection, but they can still provide reliable performance for all essential microwave tasks.

**What is CFM and why is it important for an OTR microwave?**
CFM stands for Cubic Feet per Minute, and it measures the airflow capacity of the microwave's built-in vent fan. It's crucial because it indicates how effectively the fan can pull smoke, steam, and cooking odors out of the kitchen air. A higher CFM rating means more powerful ventilation. A minimum of 300 CFM is recommended for most kitchens to maintain good air quality.

**What is the standard size for an over-the-range microwave?**
The industry standard width for an over-the-range microwave is 30 inches. This is designed to fit perfectly above a standard 30-inch-wide cooking range or cooktop. While the width is standardized, the height and depth can vary slightly between models, so it's always essential to check the detailed specifications before purchasing to ensure a proper fit with your cabinetry.
