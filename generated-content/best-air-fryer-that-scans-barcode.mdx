### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Air fryers vary significantly in price. You can find excellent entry-level models between $60 and $90. These typically have smaller capacities (4-5 quarts) and basic controls. Mid-range models between $100 and $160 often include dual baskets, higher heat capabilities (up to 450F), and premium coatings like ceramic. Premium multi-cookers and large toaster-oven styles can range from $180 to $250, offering massive capacities and advanced smart features.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look closely at the wattage and temperature range. A 1500-1800 watt machine will heat up much faster than a 1200 watt model. The temperature range is also vital; if you want to dehydrate food, you need a machine that can go as low as 90-105F. If you want maximum crunch, look for a model that hits 450F. Presets are helpful for beginners, but experienced cooks may prefer precise manual controls.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Pay attention to the basket coating. Ceramic is generally more durable and healthier than traditional PTFE/PFOA nonstick coatings, which can flake over time. The feel of the buttons and the sturdiness of the handle are good indicators of build quality. Stainless steel exteriors are more durable and easier to clean than cheap plastic housings.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Buying from established brands like Ninja, Cosori, or Chefman ensures that you have access to customer support and replacement parts. These companies often provide extensive recipe books and online communities that help you get the most out of your appliance. Established brands also tend to have better safety testing and UL/cETL certifications.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Measure your counter space and the height of your upper cabinets before buying. Some models, like the Emeril Lagasse 26QT, are quite tall and might not fit under standard cabinets. If you have limited space, consider the Ninja Flip, which can be stored upright, or the Ninja Crispi, which uses nesting glass containers to save room.
