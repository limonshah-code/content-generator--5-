## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Feiss Post Lanterns

Choosing the right post lantern involves more than just picking a style you like. To ensure you get a fixture that is beautiful, functional, and long-lasting, consider these key factors.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you buy, assess where the lantern will be installed. Measure the diameter of your existing post or pier mount to ensure compatibility; most Feiss lanterns fit a standard 3-inch diameter post. Consider the scale of your home and landscape. A large, grand home can support a larger lantern, while a smaller fixture is better suited for a modest ranch or cottage. Think about the purpose: is it for lighting a long driveway, accenting a garden path, or illuminating your front entrance? The use case will influence the size and brightness you need.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Feiss post lanterns are known for their quality, and prices reflect that. You can expect to find options ranging from approximately $150 to over $400. The price is influenced by the size of the lantern, the materials used (e.g., stainless steel vs. aluminum), and the complexity of the design and finish. Set a realistic budget, but remember that investing in a higher-quality, more durable fixture can save you money in the long run by avoiding premature replacement.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Pay close attention to the technical details. The material—be it aluminum, steel, or another metal—will determine the lantern's durability and weight. The type of glass is also crucial; clear beveled glass offers a crisp, traditional look and bright light, while seeded glass provides a textured, vintage feel with a softer glow. Check the wattage and number of bulbs to ensure it provides adequate light for your needs. Finally, always look for a UL or ETL listing for "Wet Locations," which certifies the fixture is safe for outdoor use in all weather.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
A good post lantern is an investment that should withstand the elements for years. Look for robust construction with solid-feeling materials. Powder-coated or multi-layered finishes are more resistant to chipping, fading, and rust. Aluminum is an excellent choice as it's lightweight and naturally rust-resistant. 3.  **Consider Light Output and Quality:** Think about how much light you need. A three-bulb lantern with 60-watt capacity will cast a significant amount of light, ideal for primary walkways. While Feiss's aluminum lanterns are highly durable and rust-resistant, if you live in a coastal area with salty air, upgrading to a stainless steel model like the Martinsville could be a wise long-term investment.
