## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Pre Made Fans

Choosing the right pre-made fans is crucial for efficiency and client satisfaction. Here are the key factors to consider to ensure you make the best purchase for your needs.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you buy, consider the styles your clients request most often. Do they prefer a natural, fluffy look (3D-6D fans), a full volume set (8D-14D), or a dramatic mega volume effect (16D-24D)? Also, consider the curl type. A 'C' curl provides a natural lift, while a 'D' curl offers a more dramatic, lifted look. Starting with mixed-length trays is a great way to experiment and have the versatility to create custom looks for different eye shapes.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($15-$35 ranges)
Pre-made fans are available at various price points, typically ranging from $15 to $35 per box. However, the most important metric is the cost per fan. A box with 1000 fans for $30 (3 cents per fan) is a better value than a box with 300 fans for $20 (6.7 cents per fan). High-volume salons should look for bulk options to minimize costs, while beginners or those testing a new style might prefer a smaller, more affordable box.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Pay close attention to three main technical aspects:
1.  **Material:** High-quality Korean PBT (Polybutylene Terephthalate) is the industry standard. It's soft, lightweight, holds its curl, and has a beautiful matte or satin finish.
2.  **Base Type:** Look for fans with a thin, pointy base. This allows for a stronger, more seamless bond with the natural lash and uses less adhesive. Short stem fans are great for creating a dark eyeliner effect.
3.  **Thickness:** For multi-lash fans (10D and up), the individual lash thickness should be very fine, such as 0.03mm, 0.05mm, or 0.07mm, to ensure the fan is not too heavy for the natural lash.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
The best pre-made fans are handmade for consistency and quality control. Check if the fans are heat-bonded or have a minimal amount of glue at the base. Heat-bonded fans are often more reliable as they won't fall apart when you pick them up. Durability also refers to curl retention. Look for brands that mention using a double-heating or baking process to ensure the curl remains stable for the entire life of the extension.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
While there are many brands on the market, established names like FADLASH, WENDY LASHES, and TDANCE often provide more consistent quality. They have thousands of reviews from professional lash artists, which can give you confidence in your purchase. Reading recent reviews can alert you to any changes in quality or manufacturing.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Consider your workspace. Do you prefer loose fans in a multi-compartment case or fans neatly arranged on strips in a tray? Loose fans can be faster for some artists, but trays are often easier to keep organized and are more portable for mobile technicians. Cases with separate, detachable grids for each length offer a good compromise between the two.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy pre made fans?

Absolutely. For any lash artist, time is money, and investing in high-quality pre-made fans is one of the wisest business decisions you can make. The primary advantage is the dramatic reduction in application time. Creating a volume fan by hand requires skill, precision, and several seconds per fan. Pre-made fans eliminate this step entirely, allowing you to complete a full volume set in a similar timeframe to a classic set. This means you can fit more clients into your day, significantly increasing your revenue potential.

Compared to the alternative of hand-making fans, pre-mades offer unparalleled consistency. Every fan is perfectly symmetrical with even spacing, something that is difficult to achieve by hand, especially at the end of a long day. This leads to a cleaner, more professional-looking final result. Furthermore, it reduces the physical strain on your hands and wrists, which can be a long-term benefit for your career. While the upfront cost per fan may be slightly higher than the cost of loose lashes, the time saved and the ability to serve more clients provides a massive return on investment, making pre-made fans a smart and profitable choice.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose pre made fans?

Choosing the right pre-made fans involves a methodical approach that balances client needs, your skill level, and business goals. Here is a step-by-step guide to making the best choice.

1.  **Assess Your Clientele's Needs:** Start by analyzing what your clients typically ask for. Are they looking for subtle enhancement or bold drama? This will guide your choice of dimension (e.g., 5D for natural, 20D for mega volume) and curl (C for natural, D for dramatic).

2.  **Start with a Mixed Tray:** If you are new to pre-made fans or a specific brand, always purchase a mixed-length tray first. This allows you to experiment with different lengths (e.g., 9mm to 16mm) on various eye shapes without committing to a large stock of single-length trays.

3.  **Prioritize the Base:** The base of the fan is critical. Always opt for thin, pointy bases. They create the most seamless attachment point to the natural lash, require less adhesive, and result in better retention.

4.  **Read Reviews and Check Material:** Stick to brands with positive professional reviews. Ensure the material is high-quality Korean PBT, which is the industry standard for softness and curl retention.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Ignoring Lash Health:** Never apply a fan that is too heavy for the client's natural lash. A 20D fan made of 0.03mm lashes can be lighter than a 5D fan made of 0.10mm lashes.
*   **Buying in Bulk Blindly:** Don't purchase a massive quantity of a product you've never tried before. Test a smaller box first to ensure you like the quality and ease of use.

**When to Upgrade vs. Budget Options:**
Use budget-friendly bulk options (like those from FADLASH or WENDY LASHES) for your everyday, standard volume sets. Consider investing in premium, heat-bonded fans (like TDANCE) for clients who are willing to pay more for a luxury service or for creating competition-style sets where precision is paramount.

## FAQ

**What is the best pre made fans for beginners?**
For beginners, the best pre made fans are those with a thin, pointy base and a lower dimension, such as 3D, 5D, or 10D. A mixed-length tray is ideal as it allows you to practice creating different styles. Brands like FADLASH and Eyesy Lash are very user-friendly, as their bases are easy to work with and help build application confidence quickly.

**How much should I spend on pre made fans?**
You can expect to spend between $15 and $35 per box. The most important factor is the cost per fan. Budget-friendly options can be as low as 3-4 cents per fan in large bulk boxes, while premium or specialty fans might be closer to 8-10 cents per fan. Focus on finding the best quality within your budget.

**What features are most important in pre made fans?**
The three most important features are the material, the base, and the curl stability. Look for fans made from soft, lightweight Korean PBT. The base should be thin and pointy for seamless application and good retention. Finally, ensure the brand is known for stable curls that won't relax or straighten over time.

**How do I maintain my pre made fans?**
If you buy loose fans, keep them in their original compartmentalized case to prevent them from getting mixed or damaged. Store the case in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight, as heat and humidity can affect the curl. For fans on strips, simply keep the tray closed when not in use.

**Are expensive pre made fans worth it?**
They can be. More expensive fans, like those from TDANCE, often feature heat-bonded bases that guarantee they won't fall apart, or they may use a more luxurious grade of PBT silk. While many budget-friendly options offer fantastic quality, premium fans can provide an edge in consistency and ease of use for artists who value that precision.

**What brands make the best pre made fans?**
Several brands are highly regarded by professionals. FADLASH and WENDY LASHES are known for their incredible value and reliable quality, making them great for everyday use. TDANCE is recognized for its premium, durable heat-bonded fans. YANTAISIYU and Kolybel also offer excellent specialty options for different volume effects.

**How long do pre made fans typically last?**
When applied correctly, pre-made fans last just as long as classic or hand-made volume extensions. Retention depends on the application technique, the quality of the adhesive, and the client's natural lash cycle and aftercare. Typically, a set will last 4-6 weeks before a fill is needed.

**Can I find good pre made fans on a budget?**
Absolutely. The market is full of high-quality, budget-friendly options. Brands like FADLASH offer boxes with up to 1000 fans, bringing the cost per fan down to just a few cents. WENDY LASHES is another brand that provides excellent, cost-effective solutions without compromising on the soft feel and stable curl that professionals require.
