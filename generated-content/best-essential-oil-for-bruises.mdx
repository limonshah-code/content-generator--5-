## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Essential Oil For Bruises

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before making a purchase, identify how you plan to use the oil. If you need something for immediate use on the go, a pre-diluted roll-on is the best choice. However, if you are looking to treat large areas of the body, a bulk bottle of arnica oil or a concentrated balm will be more efficient. Consider your skin type as well; those with dry skin might benefit more from a beeswax-based salve, while those with oily skin should look for lightweight oils like jojoba-based serums.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($10-$60 ranges)
Essential oils vary significantly in price based on the rarity of the plant. Helichrysum is often more expensive because it takes a large amount of flowers to produce a small amount of oil, typically ranging from $15 to $50 for a small bottle. Arnica is generally more affordable, with large 8-ounce bottles available in the $10 to $20 range. High-quality oils should be stored in dark amber or cobalt blue glass bottles to protect the contents from light and oxidation. ### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
In the world of essential oils, brand reputation is paramount. Look for brands that provide GCMS (Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry) testing results, which verify the chemical purity of the oil. If you plan to use the oil at the gym or while traveling, stick to 10ml to 30ml bottles. For home use, a 4-ounce or 8-ounce bottle offers better value.
