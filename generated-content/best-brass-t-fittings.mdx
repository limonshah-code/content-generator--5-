## What Is the Best Way to Choose brass t fittings?

Choosing the best brass T fitting involves a systematic approach to ensure you get the right part for your specific needs. Follow these steps for a successful selection.

**1. Define Your Application:**
First, clearly identify what you are connecting. Is it a rigid pipe, a flexible hose, or a combination? What fluid or gas will pass through it (air, water, fuel, oil)? This initial step dictates the fundamental type of fitting you need. For potable water, you must select a lead-free fitting.

**2. Determine the Correct Size and Type:**
- **For Threaded Pipe:** Use calipers or a ruler to measure the inner diameter of your pipe. Match this to the nominal NPT size (e.g., 1/2 inch pipe uses a 1/2 inch NPT fitting). Determine the required gender for each of the three ports on the tee (Male or Female).
- **For Flexible Hose:** Measure the inner diameter of the hose. This will correspond to the barb size you need. Ensure the barbs on the fitting match your hose's inner diameter for a snug fit.

**3. Check Pressure and Temperature Requirements:**
Review the specifications of your system. An air compressor system typically runs at 90-125 PSI, so a fitting rated for at least 150 PSI is recommended. Hydraulic systems may require much higher ratings. Similarly, ensure the fitting's operating temperature range is suitable for your environment.

**4. Inspect for Quality:**
When possible, physically inspect the fitting. Look for solid, heavy construction. The threads or barbs should be well-machined, smooth, and free of defects. Reputable brands generally have better quality control.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- **Mismatching Threads:** Do not try to connect an NPT fitting with a different thread standard (like British Standard Pipe or BSP). This will result in leaks.
- **Ignoring Lead Content:** Never use a standard brass fitting for drinking water lines. Always look for lead-free certification (e.g., ASTM B-584-06).
- **Over-tightening:** NPT threads are tapered and seal by deformation. Over-tightening can crack the female fitting. Use thread sealant and tighten until it is snug, then give it another one to two full turns.

**Upgrade vs. Budget Options:**
For critical systems (air brakes, high-pressure lines, permanent plumbing), it is always worth investing in a premium, certified fitting. For general, low-pressure workshop use or temporary setups, a quality budget option can be sufficient.
