Your primary sleeping position is the most important factor. Side sleepers generally need a softer mattress (plush to medium) to cushion their shoulders and hips. Stomach sleepers require a firm surface to prevent their hips from sinking, which can strain the lower back. Also, consider if you sleep with a partner; in that case, motion isolation becomes a key feature to look for.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Serta and Sealy offer a wide range of mattresses at various price points. You can find entry-level models in the $500-$900 range, which are great for guest rooms or budget-conscious shoppers. Premium and luxury models can exceed $1,800, providing the latest in sleep technology, superior materials, and maximum durability.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Understanding the terminology is vital. Gel memory foam is designed to contour to your body while pulling heat away. Encased or pocketed coils move independently, which is excellent for motion isolation and targeted support. A hybrid mattress combines foam layers on top of a coil system, offering the benefits of both. Zoned support means the mattress is firmer in certain areas (like the center third) to provide extra support where you need it most.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Look for mattresses with CertiPUR-US certified foams, which means they are made without harmful chemicals and are built to last. For innerspring or hybrid models, a higher coil count can indicate better support and durability. The warranty is also a good indicator of a manufacturer's confidence in their product; both Serta and Sealy typically offer a 10-year limited warranty on their mattresses.

To maintain your mattress, use a waterproof mattress protector to guard against spills and allergens. ### What is the difference between a hybrid and a foam mattress?
A foam mattress is made entirely of foam layers, such as memory foam and polyfoam. It excels at pressure relief and motion isolation. Hybrids offer the benefits of both types, providing the contouring of foam with the bounce, support, and airflow of innersprings.

### Do bed-in-a-box mattresses need time to expand?
Yes, all bed-in-a-box mattresses, including those from Serta and Sealy, need time to fully expand and off-gas. This process typically takes anywhere from 24 to 72 hours. While you can often sleep on them after a few hours, they won't reach their full height and intended firmness level until they have completely decompressed. It's best to let it expand in a well-ventilated room.
