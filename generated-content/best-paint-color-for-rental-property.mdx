## What Is the Best Way to Choose paint color for rental property?

Choosing the best paint solutions for a rental property is a strategic process. Following these steps can help you make an efficient and effective choice.

**1. Assess the Scope of the Job:**
Start by identifying what you need to accomplish. Is it a small chip in a porcelain sink, a scuff on a white wall, a faded outdoor railing, or a legal requirement? The nature of the task will immediately narrow down your options. Small interior scuffs point to touch-up kits, while outdoor projects require weather-resistant bulk paints.

**2. Prioritize Convenience and Efficiency:**
In a rental setting, time is money. Look for products that minimize prep and cleanup. All-in-one bottle-and-brush systems are ideal for quick fixes. An airtight storage container like the Paint Saint makes future touch-ups instantaneous. Avoid solutions that require multiple steps, tools, and a lengthy cleanup process for minor repairs.

**3. Address Color Matching Proactively:**
Don't wait for a problem to arise before thinking about paint colors. It's wise to purchase a multi-shade white touch-up kit to have on hand, as it will likely match most of the trim and walls in a standard rental. When you do a full paint job, always save the exact color information (brand, name, code) and store a small amount in a dedicated touch-up container.

**4. Consider Durability and Maintenance:**
Choose paints known for their durability. A washable, scuff-resistant finish will hold up better to tenants and reduce the frequency of repairs. For outdoor applications, UV and water resistance are non-negotiable to prevent frequent repainting.

**5. Don't Forget Legal and Safety Requirements:**
If your property was built before 1978, a Lead-Based Paint Disclosure Form is a legal necessity, not an option. For all interior painting, choosing low-odor, low-VOC paints is a responsible choice for the health and comfort of your tenants.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- **Guessing the Color:** Never assume you can find the right white at the store. Use a multi-kit or a color-matching tool.
- **Using Interior Paint Outside:** Interior paint will quickly fail when exposed to the elements. Always use paint specifically formulated for outdoor use.
- **Ignoring Surface Prep:** Even for small touch-ups, wiping the area clean will ensure the new paint adheres properly.
