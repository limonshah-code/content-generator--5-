## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Microwave That Is Also An Air Fryer

Choosing the right multi-function appliance requires careful consideration of your needs, space, and budget. Use this guide to help you navigate the key factors and make a confident decision.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you start shopping, think about how you'll use the appliance. Are you a large family that needs to cook whole chickens and large casseroles, or an individual in a small apartment? If you plan to air fry frequently, look for models with dedicated accessories like crisper trays. For those who love baking, a powerful convection function with precise temperature control is essential. Consider the types of meals you cook most often to prioritize the features that matter to you.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Microwave air fryer combos can range significantly in price. Basic, compact countertop models typically fall between $150 and $250. Mid-range options with more features like inverter technology and larger capacities usually cost between $250 and $450. Premium and built-in models with advanced features like smart connectivity and superior materials can exceed $500. Set a realistic budget based on the features you truly need to avoid overspending.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look beyond the basic functions. Inverter technology provides more even cooking and defrosting compared to traditional microwaves. A powerful convection element (1400W or higher) is crucial for effective baking and roasting. For air frying, look for features like 360° air circulation and included racks. Also consider wattage (1000W is a good standard), the number of power levels, and whether it has sensor cooking, which automatically adjusts time and power.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
An appliance you use daily should be built to last. Look for models with a stainless steel interior, which is more durable and easier to clean than plastic. Check for a sturdy door mechanism—features like a soft-close door are a sign of premium construction. Read user reviews to gauge the long-term reliability of the model and brand you're considering. A solid warranty (at least one year) also provides peace of mind.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Stick with well-known brands like Breville, Toshiba, Samsung, and Commercial Chef. These companies have a long history of producing reliable kitchen appliances and generally offer better customer support and easier access to replacement parts. While lesser-known brands might offer attractive prices, they can be a gamble in terms of long-term performance and service.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Measure your available counter space carefully before buying. Note the external dimensions (width, depth, and height) of the microwave and ensure you have enough clearance for ventilation, especially around the back and sides. Consider the internal capacity (measured in cubic feet). A 1.0-1.2 cu. ft. model is suitable for individuals or couples, while families will likely need 1.5 cu. ft. or more. For a truly space-saving solution, an over-the-range (OTR) model is an excellent choice if your kitchen layout allows for it.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy microwave that is also an air fryer?

Investing in a microwave that is also an air fryer is a very wise decision for the modern kitchen. The primary benefit is space efficiency. Instead of cluttering your countertop with a separate microwave, air fryer, and potentially a convection toaster oven, you get all that functionality in a single footprint. This consolidation is invaluable, especially in smaller kitchens, apartments, or for anyone who values a minimalist aesthetic.

Beyond saving space, these combo units offer enhanced culinary versatility. You can seamlessly switch from defrosting chicken in the microwave to roasting it with the convection function and finally crisping the skin with the air fryer—all in one appliance. This opens up new cooking possibilities and can even speed up meal preparation through combination cooking modes that use multiple heating elements at once. While a standalone air fryer might be slightly more powerful in some niche cases, the performance of high-quality combo units is now so advanced that the difference is negligible for most everyday tasks. The convenience, space-saving design, and expanded cooking capabilities make it a smart, long-term investment that simplifies your life and elevates your cooking.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose microwave that is also an air fryer?

Choosing the best microwave air fryer combo involves a strategic approach. Follow these steps to ensure you select the perfect appliance for your home.

1.  **Assess Your Space and Needs:** First, measure the area where the appliance will go. Decide if you need a countertop, over-the-range, or built-in model. Then, list your primary uses. Will you be air frying daily? Do you need a large capacity for family meals? Is smart home integration a priority? Answering these questions will narrow your search significantly.

2.  **Set Your Budget:** Determine how much you are willing to spend. This will help you focus on models within your price range, preventing you from being tempted by features you may not need. Remember to balance cost with quality and desired features.

3.  **Prioritize Key Features:** Focus on the non-negotiables. For many, this includes inverter technology for even cooking, a minimum of 1000 watts for microwave power, and a high-wattage convection element for true baking performance. Check for user-friendly controls, easy-to-clean interiors (stainless steel is best), and useful presets.

4.  **Read Reviews and Compare Models:** Once you have a shortlist, dive into user reviews and professional comparisons. Pay attention to comments on reliability, noise level, and how well the air fryer function performs. This real-world feedback is invaluable.

5.  **Avoid Common Mistakes:** A common mistake is buying a unit that is too large or too small for your needs. Another is prioritizing gimmicky features over core performance. Don't be swayed by a dozen functions if the basic microwave and air fryer performance is weak. Finally, avoid models without a warranty from a reputable brand.

Upgrading from a simple microwave to a combo unit is a great move when your old appliance fails or when you're looking to declutter your kitchen. For those on a tight budget, a solid mid-range model from a brand like Toshiba often provides the best balance of price and performance, offering features found in more premium units without the hefty price tag.

**What is the best microwave that is also an air fryer for beginners?**

This takes the guesswork out of cooking and makes it easy to get started with both microwave and air fryer functions without a steep learning curve.

**How much should I spend on microwave that is also an air fryer?**
You can expect to spend between $150 and $450 for a quality countertop model. Budget-friendly options are typically under $250, while mid-range models with advanced features like inverter technology fall in the $250-$450 range. Premium or large built-in models can cost $500 or more, offering superior build quality and smart features.

**What features are most important in microwave that is also an air fryer?**
The most important features are a powerful convection heating element for true baking and roasting, effective air circulation for crispy results, and inverter technology for even microwave cooking and defrosting. A stainless steel interior for durability and ease of cleaning, along with intuitive controls and a decent capacity, are also crucial.

**How do I maintain my microwave that is also an air fryer?**
To maintain your appliance, wipe down the interior with a damp cloth after each use to prevent food buildup. For greasy splatters, use a microwave-safe cleaner or a solution of water and vinegar. Regularly wash the turntable, grill racks, and crisper trays. Using the built-in odor removal function, if available, can also help keep it fresh.

**Are expensive microwave that is also an air fryer worth it?**
Expensive models are often worth it if you value advanced features, superior performance, and better build quality. Premium appliances like the Breville Combi Wave offer features like the Element IQ system for precision cooking and are significantly quieter. If you are a passionate home cook or want a durable, long-lasting appliance, the investment can be justified.

**What brands make the best microwave that is also an air fryer?**
Several reputable brands excel in this category. Breville is known for its premium, high-performance models. Toshiba offers a wide range of reliable and feature-rich options at various price points, making them a popular choice. Samsung is a leader in smart technology integration, while brands like Commercial Chef and AAOBOSI offer excellent specialty models like over-the-range and built-in units.

**How long do microwave that is also an air fryer typically last?**
With proper care and regular cleaning, a good quality microwave air fryer combo should last between 7 to 10 years. The lifespan can be affected by the frequency of use and the overall build quality of the unit. Choosing a model from a reputable brand with a good warranty can help ensure its longevity.

**Can I find good microwave that is also an air fryer on a budget?**
Yes, absolutely. Brands like Toshiba and Magic Chef offer excellent budget-friendly models that provide great multi-functionality without breaking the bank. The TOSHIBA 6-in-1 Inverter model, for example, includes premium features like inverter technology at a very competitive price, making it a fantastic value.

**Does an air fryer microwave combo cook as well as a standalone air fryer?**
Modern, high-quality combo units cook remarkably well, often on par with standalone air fryers for most common foods like fries, wings, and vegetables. A dedicated air fryer might have a slight edge in airflow for very specific tasks, but the convenience and performance of a top-tier combo unit make the difference negligible for the average user.

**What is the difference between convection and air frying in these combo units?**
Both use a fan to circulate hot air, but they are optimized differently. Convection cooking is designed for baking and roasting, using broader, even heat to cook food through. Air frying uses a more intense, high-velocity blast of hot air from the top to create a crispy, fried texture on the food's surface, mimicking a deep fryer with far less oil.
