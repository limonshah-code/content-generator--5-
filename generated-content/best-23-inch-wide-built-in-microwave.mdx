## What to Look for Before Buying the Best 23 Inch Wide Built In Microwave

1. ### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
   Before purchasing, measure your cabinet cutout dimensions precisely. While many of these are listed as 24-inch models, they are designed to fit cutouts that are typically 23 to 23.5 inches wide. Determine if you need a standard swinging door or a drawer-style microwave, which is better for under-counter accessibility.

2. ### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($400-$1,200)
   Basic built-in microwaves usually start around $400 to $600. If you are looking for advanced features like 3D convection, air frying, or motorized drawer mechanisms, expect to spend between $800 and $1,200. Always factor in the cost of a matching trim kit if it is not included.

3. ### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
   Look for a minimum of 1000 watts for efficient heating. Sensor cooking is a must-have for those who reheat food frequently, as it prevents overcooking. If you plan on using it for more than just reheating, look for convection modes and multiple power levels to handle delicate tasks like melting chocolate.

4. ### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
   Stainless steel interiors are generally more durable and easier to clean than painted ones. Check for reinforced door hinges and thick tempered glass. Brands with solid warranties and local support teams provide better long-term security for your investment.

5. ### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
   Established brands like Frigidaire and COSMO have wider availability for replacement parts and specialized service technicians. Newer brands often provide more features for a lower price, so read recent user reviews to gauge their long-term reliability.

6. ### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
   Unlike countertop models, built-in units require venting considerations. Ensure there is enough clearance behind and above the unit for heat dissipation. Drawer models are excellent for saving counter space but require a deep enough cabinet to accommodate the slide mechanism.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy 23 inch wide built in microwave?

Investing in a built-in microwave is an excellent decision for anyone looking to maximize kitchen space and achieve a high-end, integrated look. Unlike countertop models that clutter your workspace, a built-in unit is tucked away into cabinetry, providing a cleaner aesthetic and more room for food preparation. These units are often built to higher standards of durability and performance compared to cheap portable alternatives.

The long-term benefits include increased home value, as integrated appliances are highly desirable to potential buyers. While the initial cost is higher due to installation and specialized hardware, the added functionality of convection and sensor technology often allows these units to function as a secondary oven. This versatility is invaluable during holidays or large family gatherings. For most modern homeowners, the combination of sleek design and advanced cooking capabilities makes it a very wise long-term investment.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose 23 inch wide built in microwave?

Choosing the right unit involves a systematic approach to ensure it fits both your kitchen and your cooking style. Start by measuring your available space at least three times to avoid expensive return shipping. Once you have the dimensions, decide between a drawer-style or a wall-mount unit. Drawers are better for accessibility and universal design, while wall-mount units are more traditional and often offer larger capacities.

Step-by-step selection guide:
- Determine your primary use (reheating vs. full cooking).
- Select a power level (at least 1000W is recommended).
- Check if your cabinetry requires a specific trim kit.
- Evaluate safety features like child locks and cool-touch doors.
- Compare warranties and brand reputations.
- Avoid common mistakes like ignoring the depth of the unit, which can prevent flush installation. If you are on a budget, look for models with standard features but high reliability ratings.

## FAQ

**What is the best 23 inch wide built in microwave for beginners?**
For beginners, the Frigidaire Gallery is an excellent choice. It features an intuitive control panel and highly accurate sensor cooking that removes the need for manual timing. The straightforward design and reliable performance make it easy to use right out of the box without a steep learning curve.

**How much should I spend on 23 inch wide built in microwave?**
A budget of $500 to $800 is standard for a high-quality unit. Models in this range typically offer sensor technology and stainless steel finishes. If you want specialized features like air frying or drawer mechanisms, you should be prepared to spend closer to $1,000 or more.

**What features are most important in 23 inch wide built in microwave?**
Sensor cooking is the most vital feature for daily convenience as it prevents dry food. Convection heating is also important if you want the microwave to act as a second oven. Finally, a child safety lock and a soft-close door add significant value to the user experience.

**How do I maintain my 23 inch wide built in microwave?**
Regularly wipe down the interior with a damp cloth and mild detergent to prevent grease buildup. For stainless steel exteriors, use a dedicated cleaner to maintain the shine. Ensure the venting areas are free of dust to prevent overheating and maintain the longevity of the electrical components.

**Are expensive 23 inch wide built in microwave worth it?**
Yes, they often include convection, air frying, and better insulation. High-end models also use superior materials like tempered glass and reinforced hinges that last longer than budget models. The integrated look they provide also adds more value to your kitchen than a cheaper countertop unit would.

**What brands make the best 23 inch wide built in microwave?**
Frigidaire, COSMO, and AAOBOSI are currently leading the market. Frigidaire is known for massive capacity and reliability. COSMO offers beautiful modern designs, while AAOBOSI provides excellent multi-functional appliances that combine air frying with microwave technology at a great price point.

**How long do 23 inch wide built in microwave typically last?**
A well-maintained built-in microwave typically lasts between 7 and 10 years. Because they are protected by cabinetry and built with more robust components than portable models, they often outlast their countertop counterparts. Regular cleaning and proper ventilation are key to reaching the 10-year mark.

**Can I find good 23 inch wide built in microwave on a budget?**
Yes, models like the Gasland 24-inch offer convection and sensor features at a lower price than premium drawer models. You can save money by choosing a standard door-swing model rather than a motorized drawer, as the mechanical complexity of drawers often drives up the price.
