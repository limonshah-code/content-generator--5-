The price for a 5-cup white coffee maker can range from around $20 for a very basic model to upwards of $70 for one with programmable features and a premium design. A reliable, no-frills machine like the Brentwood can be found for under $25. Mid-range options with features like auto-pause and a reusable filter typically fall between $30 and $50. ### Key Features - Most important technical specifications

*   **Auto Shut-Off:** A crucial safety feature that turns the warming plate off after a set period. This provides peace of mind.
*   **Auto Pause (Grab-a-Cup):** Allows you to sneak a cup mid-brew without making a mess. Almost a standard feature, but always check for it.
*   **Reusable Filter:** An eco-friendly and cost-effective feature that eliminates the need for paper filters.
*   **Removable Water Reservoir:** Makes filling the machine much easier and less prone to spills.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Look for a sturdy carafe, preferably made of thick, thermal-shock-resistant glass. Check that the plastic housing feels solid and not flimsy. ### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
The primary appeal of a 5-cup coffee maker is its compact size. Measure your available counter space before buying. Pay attention to the machine's footprint (width and depth) and height, especially if you plan to place it under cabinets. Models like the Brentwood and Mr. Coffee Mini Brew are exceptionally compact, making them perfect for tiny kitchens, dorms, or even for travel in an RV.
