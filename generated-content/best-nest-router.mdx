## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Nest Router

Choosing the right Nest router system involves more than just picking the newest model. To ensure you get the perfect fit for your home and lifestyle, consider these crucial factors.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, assess the size and layout of your home. A single router might be perfect for a 1,500 sq. ft. apartment, but a multi-story 4,000 sq. ft. house will need a multi-point mesh system to eliminate dead zones. Also, consider your internet usage. A family of gamers and 4K streamers will benefit from the high-end performance of the Nest Wifi Pro (Wi-Fi 6E), while a household that primarily browses the web and streams HD video will be perfectly happy with the standard Nest Wifi (Wi-Fi 5). Finally, note that the Nest Wifi Pro line is not backward compatible with older Google Wifi or Nest Wifi systems, so you cannot mix and match them.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose nest router?

Choosing the right Nest router system is a straightforward process if you follow a few key steps. By carefully considering your specific needs, you can avoid overspending or under-buying.

**1. Measure Your Home's Square Footage:** The first step is to know how much area you need to cover. A single router covers up to 2,200 sq. ft. A router and one point cover up to 3,800 sq. ft. A router and two points can handle up to 5,400 sq. ft. Choose the pack that best matches your home's size. It's better to have slightly more coverage than not enough.

**2. Count Your Connected Devices:** Make a mental list of everything that connects to your Wi-Fi: phones, laptops, smart TVs, gaming consoles, speakers, cameras, etc. If you have over 50 devices, you'll benefit more from the power of a Nest Wifi or Nest Wifi Pro system, which can handle 100+ connections gracefully.

**3. Check Your Internet Plan Speed:** If you pay for gigabit internet (1,000 Mbps or more), you need a Nest Wifi Pro (Wi-Fi 6E) system to get the full benefit of those speeds. If your plan is 500 Mbps or less, the standard Nest Wifi (Wi-Fi 5) system will be more than sufficient and will save you money.

**4. Decide on Smart Features:** Do you want your Wi-Fi extenders to also function as smart speakers? If so, choose the standard Nest Wifi system where the 'Points' have Google Assistant built-in. The Nest Wifi Pro units are all identical routers and do not have integrated speakers.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Mixing Systems:** Do not try to mix Nest Wifi Pro (Wi-Fi 6E) units with older Nest Wifi or Google Wifi (Wi-Fi 5) units. They are not compatible and will not form a mesh network together.
*   **Poor Placement:** Avoid placing your router or points inside cabinets, behind TVs, or near large metal objects. They work best when placed out in the open, about waist-high.
*   **Buying Too Few Points:** Don't try to stretch the coverage of one or two units too far in a large or oddly shaped house. It's better to have one more point than you think you need for a truly seamless connection.
