## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Portable Rv Toilets

Choosing the right portable toilet involves more than just picking one off the shelf. Consider these key factors to ensure you find a model that perfectly suits your travel needs and provides comfort and convenience on the road.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, think about how you'll be using the toilet. Are you a solo van lifer, a family in a large RV, or a weekend tent camper? For tight spaces like a van, a compact model like the **Thetford Porta Potti 365** is ideal. For long trips with multiple people, a high-capacity model like the **Kohree 5.8 Gallon** will minimize emptying chores. ### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Pay close attention to a few key specs.
*   **Tank Capacity:** Waste tanks typically range from 2.5 to 5.8 gallons. A larger tank means less frequent emptying. A 5-gallon tank is a good all-around size for a couple on a weekend trip.
*   **Flush Mechanism:** Piston pumps (Thetford) generally provide a more powerful flush than simpler bellow pumps (Camco). Battery-powered or pressurized systems offer the most vigorous clean.
*   **Seals and Valves:** A high-quality slide valve is crucial for preventing leaks and controlling odors. Look for models praised for their tight, secure seals.
*   **Indicators:** A level indicator for the waste tank is an incredibly useful feature that takes the guesswork out of emptying.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Your portable toilet needs to withstand the bumps and movements of travel. Look for models made from high-density polyethylene (HDPE) or ABS plastic, which are known for their strength and resistance to cracking. Check the weight capacity—a higher number (300+ lbs) indicates a more robust build. The latches that hold the fresh and waste water tanks together should be sturdy and easy to operate but secure enough to prevent accidental separation.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Brands like Thetford and Camco have been in the RV sanitation business for decades. Their products are often refined over many generations and backed by reliable customer service and warranties. ### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Measure the space where you plan to store and use the toilet. Note the product's dimensions (L x W x H) to ensure a good fit. Consider the toilet's weight, both empty and full. A 5.8-gallon waste tank can weigh over 40 pounds when full, so ensure you can comfortably carry it to a disposal station. If portability is your absolute priority, a foldable or highly compact model is essential.
