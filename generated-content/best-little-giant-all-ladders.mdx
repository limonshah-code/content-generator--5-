It's always better to have a ladder that is slightly too long than one that is too short.

**3. Choose Your Material:** For 99% of users, lightweight and durable aircraft-grade aluminum is the perfect choice. However, if you are an electrician or will be working anywhere near live electrical wires, you must choose a non-conductive fiberglass model like the Dark Horse. This is a critical safety decision.

**4. Consider Key Convenience Features:** Think about your workspace. Will you be moving the ladder frequently across a large area? If so, Tip & Glide wheels are a must-have. Do you work on uneven ground? Look for models with built-in ratcheting leg levelers.

If you're a painter, the Project Tray and Wall Standoff are invaluable. If you plan on large-scale work, the Trestle Brackets are essential.
