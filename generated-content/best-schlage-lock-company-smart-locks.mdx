## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Schlage Lock Company Smart Locks

Choosing the right smart lock involves more than just picking the one that looks best. Consider these key factors to ensure you select a lock that fits your home, lifestyle, and security needs perfectly.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you buy, check your door's physical specifications. Measure the door thickness (most Schlage locks fit 1-3/8 in to 1-3/4 in), the backset (the distance from the edge of the door to the center of the hole, typically 2-3/8 in or 2-3/4 in), and the bore hole diameter. Also, consider your smart home ecosystem. If you're an Apple user, a Schlage Encode Plus with HomeKit support is ideal. If you use Alexa or Google Home, most Schlage WiFi locks will integrate seamlessly. Think about your use case: do you need to manage hundreds of codes for a rental, or just a few for your family?

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Schlage smart locks offer a range of options at different price points. You can generally expect to spend between $150 and $350. Entry-level models might offer basic keyless entry via a keypad. Mid-range models, like the popular Schlage Encode series, typically add built-in WiFi for remote access and cost around $250. Premium models, like the Schlage Encode Plus, can exceed $300 and include advanced features like Apple Home Key support. Set a budget based on the features that are most important to you, like remote access or specific smart home integrations.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Several key features differentiate smart locks. **Connectivity** is crucial: built-in WiFi (like in the Encode and Arrive series) allows for direct connection to the internet for remote control without a hub. **Access Methods** are another factor; look for a combination of keypad, app control, and a traditional key for backup. **Guest Access** is a major benefit, so check the maximum number of user codes the lock can store. Features like **Auto-Lock**, which automatically secures the door after a set time, and **Built-in Alarms** that sound during a forced entry attempt add significant layers of convenience and security.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Schlage is renowned for its build quality. Look for the BHMA (Builders Hardware Manufacturers Association) certification on the packaging. A Grade 1 (or AAA) rating for Security, Durability, and Finish is the highest residential standard and ensures the lock can withstand tampering and years of use. The materials matter, too; solid metal construction is a must for an exterior lock. Also, consider the keypad type. Fingerprint-resistant touchscreens offer a sleek look, while tactile silicone buttons provide excellent all-weather reliability.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
When it comes to your home's security, brand reputation is paramount. Schlage has been a leader in the lock industry for over 100 years, building a legacy of trust and reliability. Choosing an established brand like Schlage means you are getting a product backed by extensive research, development, and security testing. It also ensures better customer support, longer warranty periods (often lifetime on mechanical parts), and ongoing app support and firmware updates to keep your lock secure against emerging threats.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
While a smart lock isn't portable, its size and fit are critical. The interior housing of a smart lock contains the motor and batteries, making it larger than a traditional thumbturn. Before purchasing, check the dimensions of the interior assembly to ensure you have enough clearance and that it won't interfere with your door's operation or another nearby lock (like on a handleset). A well-designed lock will have a profile that is functional without being overly bulky or obtrusive.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy schlage lock company smart locks?

Investing in a Schlage smart lock is a very wise decision for the modern homeowner, as it represents a significant upgrade in both convenience and security. The primary value proposition lies in moving beyond the physical key. With a smart lock, you eliminate the risk of lost or stolen keys and the costly process of rekeying your locks. Instead, you gain the ability to grant access remotely and temporarily. You can create a unique code for a dog walker that only works during their scheduled hours, let a friend in while you're at work, or give a contractor access for the duration of a project, all from your smartphone.

Compared to traditional locks, the long-term benefits are substantial. You gain a complete audit trail of who enters and exits your home, providing invaluable peace of mind. The auto-lock feature ensures your home is secure even when you forget to lock the door. While the initial cost is higher than a standard deadbolt, a Schlage smart lock should be viewed as an investment in a more secure and streamlined lifestyle. It simplifies daily routines, enhances the safety of your family, and integrates your home's most critical security point into your connected life.

Decide how much you are comfortable spending. A basic, non-connected keypad lock from Schlage will be the most affordable.
