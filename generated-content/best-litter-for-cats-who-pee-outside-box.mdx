## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Litter For Cats Who Pee Outside Box

Choosing the right products to address inappropriate urination involves understanding your cat's specific needs and the root cause of the behavior. Here’s a guide to help you select the most effective solution.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, identify the problem. Is your cat a 'high pee-er' who sprays over the side of the box? A splash guard or a high-sided/hooded box is the answer. Is your cat avoiding the box entirely? An attractant powder might be needed to lure them back. For older cats with mobility issues, a low-entry box is crucial. If the issue is messy tracking, look for low-dust pellet systems or clumping litters known for minimal tracking. Match the solution to the specific behavior for the best results.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Solutions range from affordable to premium. A simple bag of litter attractant can cost as little as $10-$15. A high-quality clumping litter typically falls in the $15-$25 range per box. A complete litter box system like the Tidy Cats Breeze can have an initial cost of $40-$60, plus ongoing costs for proprietary pads and pellets. Accessories like pee shields or floor pads are usually in the $15-$30 range. Set a budget that you're comfortable with for both the initial purchase and any recurring costs.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose litter for cats who pee outside box?

Choosing the right solution requires a methodical approach. Follow these steps to find the perfect product for your feline friend.

**Step 1: Consult Your Veterinarian**
Before changing products, rule out any medical issues. Urinary tract infections, kidney problems, or arthritis can cause a cat to avoid the litter box. A clean bill of health from your vet ensures you're treating a behavioral issue, not a medical one.

Identify the specific problem. Does your cat stand on the edge and miss? Do they refuse to step into the box at all? Do they kick litter everywhere? Are they spraying high on the walls? **Step 3: Start with a Simple Change**
Don't overhaul everything at once. Begin with the simplest solution. If you suspect your cat dislikes the current litter, try a different type. A cat attractant like **Fresh Step Litter Box Attractant** is an easy, low-cost first step to mix into your existing setup.

**Step 4: Upgrade the Litter or Box**
If an attractant doesn't work, consider upgrading the core components. Switch to a powerful odor-controlling litter like **ARM & HAMMER Clump & Seal SLIDE** to create a more inviting environment.
